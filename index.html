Selected Work

Two pieces spanning energy policy and cultural criticism, produced as part of an independent writing portfolio.

Op-Ed  ·  Energy Policy

Canada's Clean Grid Has a Rainy-Day Problem

Canada's electricity grid is among the cleanest in the world — but its low-emissions record depends heavily on hydroelectric availability. This piece examines the structural gap that emerges when hydro underperforms, and why electrification may widen that gap before it narrows it.

Essay  ·  Culture & Media

The Anatomy of Barbenheimer as a Phenomenon and Lesson

The summer of 2023 produced one of the most unexpected cultural events in recent Hollywood history. This essay examines what made "Barbenheimer" work, why it can't simply be replicated, and what its underlying logic means for the future of the blockbuster.
